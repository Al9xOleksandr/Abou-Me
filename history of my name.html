History of name Oleksandr

Homer is first mentioned in linear letters B as the second name of Paris (8th century BC). Thanks to the king of Macedonia, Alexander the Great (IV century BC), he was also known as one of the names (epithets) of the goddess Hera (the eldest daughter of Kronos (Saturn) and Rhea), who was thought to be a warrior in battle. This name comes from the Greek language: the word "alex" means "defender" and "andros" means man. So, Alexander is the one who defends. The name has become popular in many countries, so it may sound different in different languages. So if you hear, for example, the name Iskander, know that it is Turkish Alexander. And short forms in this name - a whole bunch: Sasha, Sanya, Shura, Sashko, Oles ...

Famous people with name Oleksandr

1) Alexander of Macedon (July 356, Pella, Macedonia - June 13, 323 BC, Babylon) - King of Macedonia since 336, commander, creator of the largest state of the ancient world, son of Philip II of Macedon.

2) Alexander Sergeyevich Pushkin (June 6 (May 26, old style) 1799, Moscow - February 10 (January 29, old style) 1837, St. Petersburg) - the great Russian poet, the founder of new Russian literature, the creator of modern Russian literary language.

3) Alexander II The Liberator (1818-1881). The Russian emperor, ascended the throne on February 19, 1855. The eldest son of Nicholas I. The reign of Alexander II was marked by the "liberation of the peasants" from serfdom. The peasant reform was followed by judicial, zemstvo, military. After the suppression of the Polish uprising and the shooting of Karakozov at the Tsar (April 4, 1866), Alexander II decisively moved on to the path of an openly reactionary policy. Severe persecution of the press begins, university and zemstvo reforms are being revised. After a series of unsuccessful attempts, he was killed by the Narodnaya Volya on 1 March 1881.

4) Alexander Petrovich Dovzhenko (September 11 (August 30, old)) 1894 - November 25, 1956) - Soviet film director, screenwriter, author of short stories and short stories. Honored Artist of the Ukrainian SSR (1940). People's Artist of the RSFSR (1950). Laureate of the Lenin Prize (1959 - posthumously for the literary script "Poem on the Sea").

5) Oleksandr Zinchenko (born December 15, 1996, Radomyshl, Zhytomyr region, Ukraine) is a Ukrainian footballer, midfielder and left-back of the English Manchester City and Ukraine national team.

6) Oleksandr Ousyk (born January 17, 1987, Simferopol) is a professional Ukrainian boxer. Absolute World Heavyweight Champion (WBO (2016 - 2019), WBC (2018 - 2019), IBF (2018 - 2019), WBA (Super) (2018 - 2019), The Ring (2018 - incl.)) , Olympic champion (2012), world champion (2011), European champion (2008), multiple champion of Ukraine.Honored Master of Sports of Ukraine.
